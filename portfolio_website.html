Tuesday, June 6th 2017

Curves and Flowers

Language: Scratch

While messing around with the pen function, I made a program to draw more advanced shapes than the squares and triangles we began with. I drew from my knowledge of polar curves to create flowers, spirals, and limacons. (The more interesting curves are courtesy of the internet.) The most challenging aspect of this project was mapping polar curves onto the coordinate plane, because Scratch only understands x and y. Click on the link to check out my finished project, which allows the user to select many different shapes. Enjoy!

Link: scratch.mit.edu

Wednesday, June 22nd 2017

Robot Dance Party

Language: Arduino C

After practicing some Arduino C with blinking LED lights and researching Servos, we put our knowledge together to make our robots dance! Second by second, my group choreographed a dance to the soundrack "Cory in the House". We only covered about 30 seconds of the song, but our robot's movements (spinning, rapid back-and-forth movements, and wavy motion) matched the music well. After an afternoon of programming, we presented a robot that could dance to the music!
